How are exercises ranked?

Excercises are ranked on how well they are at buidling muscle without compromising the health of your joints and body, which could lead to injury. They are also ranked specifically for that muscle, for example a back sqaut can be an A tier quads exercise but only a C tier hamstrings exercise, while being an overall good leg exercise

Tier Values

Tiers use a letter system from S meaning the best of the best and D meaning the absolute worst.

  • S-Tier: The best options out there and are essential to building muscle
  • A-Tier: good exercises worth doing
  • B-Tier: Exercises that are just okay
  • C-Tier: Not the best and can be replaced by better options
  • D-tier: Avoid these exercises

Shorthands

Within these tier list the name of some exercises may use shorthand. Below is a key to understand these shorthand abbreviations

  • BB means barbell
  • DB means dumbbell
  • HIT means high intensity training
  • AMRAP means as many reps as possilbe
  • SSB means safety sqaut bar
  • RDLs means Romanian deadlifts
  • GHD means Glute-ham developer
  • Alt means alternating
  • Sup means supinated
  • OHP means Overhead Press
